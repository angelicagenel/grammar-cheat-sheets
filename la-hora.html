LA HORA EN ESPAÑOL

Time Expressions & Questions | Spanish Learning Edge

🔤 Before You Begin...

To tell time in Spanish, students need to:

  • Know numbers 1–30 (especially 1–12 and key increments like 15, 30, and 45)
  • Use the present tense of the verb ser (es / son)

⏱️ How to Ask About Time — Two Different Questions

¿Qué hora es? ¿A qué hora es...?

Used to ask the current time.

What time is it?

→ Response: Use es / son

¿Qué hora es? What time is it?
Son las tres de la tarde. It's 3:00 PM.

Used to ask at what time something happens.

At what time is...?

→ Response: Use a la / a las

¿A qué hora es la fiesta? At what time is the party?
La fiesta es a las cinco. The party is at 5:00.

Note: Both structures deal with time, but the first asks for the time now, and the second asks for the time of an event.

🕐 Stating the Time

Es la una
It's one o'clock
Only for 1:00
Son las cuatro
It's four o'clock
For all other hours (2-12)

➕ Adding Minutes

Son las dos y diez
It's 2:10
Son las cuatro y cuarto
It's 4:15
Son las nueve y media
It's 9:30

🕓 Special Time Expressions

en punto
Son las ocho en punto
It's exactly 8:00
el mediodía
Es el mediodía
It's noon
la medianoche
Es la medianoche
It's midnight

⚠️ Less Common: Subtracting Minutes (menos)

⚠️ Note:

This is taught in many textbooks, but is less common in casual Mexican speech.

  • Son las nueve menos diez. – It's 8:50.
  • Es la una menos cuarto. – It's 12:45.

💬 Bonus: Natural Ways to Ask the Time in Mexico

¿Qué hora tienes?
Do you have the time?
¿Me das la hora?
Can you give me the time?
¿Me podrías decir la hora?
Could you tell me the time?

Note: These expressions are common in Mexico and widely understood, but the most standard and school-taught form is still ¿Qué hora es?
